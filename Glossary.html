Thadu
Thadu is a monster that king Nazayun created as a weapon. it is portrayed with the features of a crab, and snaps and pinces at Tru and Shani. he is one of the Western Fold scorpion warriors. In the story, his main impression is portrayed as a terrorizing monster who Harrassed Tru during her time in the fold.

Elang's Proposal

Elang needs Truyan in a way. Because he is banished from the Dragon Realm (See page 'Characters') the only way he is able to return to Ai'long and overthrow his father, is if he to return wed to his Heavenly match. Elang proposes that Truyan be wed to him as part of the plan, and in return, he will grant Truyan


Dragon Pearl
In Ailong, every dragon has a pearl. its it akin to a heart. it is their sorce of magic, power, and feeling.

"Lord Elang's was taken from him when he was a child because his father, the late Lord Ta'ginian, wed a human without the Dragon King's blessing." (123)

This is significant, because without Elang's Dragon Pearl, he is devoid of feelings, emotions, and his superhuman powers, rather than political power as the grandson of king Nazayun.

Government Authority

Truyan's family is not on good terms with the local government, and it becomes a pushing factor that guides most of Truyan's decisions in the book.

Renhai

Renhai is the Barladan magistrate, the local government official who oversees Tru's district. he is portrayed as a boot licker of the eimpire, a corrupt cowardly two-faced, self-serving shell of a man who's loyalty is with whoever has the most power. He bends to whoever has power.

Madam Yargui

Madam yargui is one of the most feared crime lords in Gangsun. She is constantly putting Tru's family in a hard time.
She holds a massive gambling debt over the head of Tru's mother. If the debt is not paid, she threatens to take and sell Truyan's sister, Falina. She is the selective pressure factor that more so inclines Truyan to accept Elang's deal.
