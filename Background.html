A Forgery of Fate--Background

Background:
A Forgery of Fate is a fantasy novel that takes place in a fictional world.

All of the books written by Elizabeth lim take place in the fictional world of Loryan. it is a land similar to Pangaea, but it was split into two realms. The mortal realm, and the dragon Realm.

The Mortal Realm:

the mortal realm is where all humans live. the culture of diferent places in the mortal realm are similar to customs found in Eastern and Central Asian countries (China, Japan,Korea, ect...) There have been multiple different books that peice together the societal structure that the mortal realm has. In every novel, there is an Emperor of a region, then the nobles and then the citizens and cityfold and peasants.
Truyan Lives in the Mortal Realm as a Barladan, but she is able to enter the dragon realm because she was wed to Elang'anmi, the half-dragon hybrid.

The Dragon Realm

The Dragon realm is the other half of Loryan, known as Ai'long. The lord of the dragons is king Nazayun, and he rules over not only the dragons, but all the animals within the sea. Humans are banned from entering the dragon realm, unless they are wed or invited by another dragon. Elang'anmi was banned from Ai'long, because he was a half-human, half-dragon hybrid and was seen as an abomination. His grandfather said Elang was only ever allowed to return to Ailong if he were to find his Heavenly Match, and be wed to have an heir. He was able to return when he got "married" to Truyan, as part of their deal.

The Fold

The Fold is a deep forbiddden chasm located near Yonsar's castle, which is located in the mortal realm of Loryan (see map below)
No one is allowed to go to the Fold for fear of going missing, and more than likely getting killed. When Mailoh goes missing, Tru and Shani have to go to the Fold to find him, where they meet Thadu, and must find and retrieve him.

Westerly Seas

The Westerly seas are a region that are described as empty, cold, lonely, and desolate. Within the region lies Yonsars castle, where Elang lived when he had been in the Dragon realm. Because Elang was banished, the region was left empty, with no one to inhabit it. as described, "I spied no castle, not even a gate." (182)
Truyan is led to this part of the ocean after her marriage with Elang, and her quarters are described as "more of a fortress really, for it was scarcely less dull and desolate than the seascape outside" (182)

Gangshun

Truyan's Home

in the story, Truyan gets moved around multiple places, but the one place she calls home is the coastal house near the sea. Back home, she lived in the Barladan quarter of Gangsun, a poor coastal district on the outskirts of the Gangshun Empire, with her family. It is described as:
"a ramshackle room behind the fish market, which Fal and {Tru} had rentedb ecause it was cheap and had no roaches. the landlord hadn't lied about the roaches, but instead there were mice, and a rotting stench taht wouldnt go away no matter how much incense Mama burned." (46).
The neighborhood is noisy, crowded, and full of traders, fishermen, and bandits.

Elang's Palace (Land)

When Elang had been banished from Ai'Long he had been living in the mortal realm, in a palace where he would not interact with the outside world.
Truyan meets Elang when she falls into his garden, after escaping government authority.

Undersea palace

When she is wed to Elang, she is taken to the dragon realm, where she is given a personal chamber within the ruins of Elang's palace. It serves at not a home, but a cage, where she is isolated, wathched, and constntly in danger. It is cold, lonely, and dangerous for her, despite being the wife of the grandson of the Lord of Dragons.
